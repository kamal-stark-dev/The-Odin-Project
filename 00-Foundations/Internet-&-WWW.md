# Internet

The internet is a **global, decentralized network of interconnected computers** and devices that use standard protocols (**TCP/IP**) to communicate, sharing information worldwide.

It's a massive "_network or networks_" linking millions of private, public, academic, and business networks, enabling data transfer via cables and wireless technology.

> _In simple terms_: The internet is a wire, burried in the ground. It can be a fibre cable, a copper wire, or wireless signals. But it's essentially a wire, it's useful because two or more computer/devices connected to this wire can communicate to each other.

### Key Aspects of the Internet:

- **Infrastructure**: It consists of physical cables (including underwater cables) and wireless connections (Wi-FI, satellites) that connects servers, routers, and personal devices together.

- **How it works**: Data travels in "packets" across this network, allowing instant communication across the globe.

- **Internet vs. Web**: The internet is physical infrastructure, while the World Wide Web (WWW) is a service running on top of the internet that lets users browse information via hyperlinks.

### Usage Examples of Internet:

- **Communication**: Email, instant messaging, VOIP calls, video conferencing.

- **Information Sharing/Access**: Browsing websites, using search engines, and reading online articles.

- **Entertainment**: Streaming videos, listening to music, and playing online games.

- **Commerce & Services**: Online shopping, internet banking, and social networking.

### Componenets of the Internet:

- **Client/Endpoints**: Smartphones, laptops, IoT devices.
- **Servers**: Computers that store data (websites).
- **Hardware**: Routers and switches that direct traffic (data packets).

### Difference between Internet and World Wide Web:

Internet is a **technical infrastructure** which allows billions of devices to be connected all together. Among those devices, some computers (called _Web servers_) can send messages intelligible(able to be understood) to **web browsers**.

The _Internet_ is an infrastructure, whereas the _Web_ is a service built on top of it.

It's worth noting that there are several other services built on top of the Internet, such as **email** and **IRC(Internet Relay Chat)** - chatting system.
